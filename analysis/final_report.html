Problem 1: Gibbs Sampler

We derive the Gibbs Sampler updates for class labels \(c_i\) and the mean of the \(k\)th Gaussian \(\mu_k\). The full conditional for \(C_{ik}\) i.e. the probability observation \(i\) is predicted to have mean \(\mu_k\) is

\[P(C_{ik} = 1 | y_i, \mu_k) = \frac{P(y_i, \mu_k | C_{ik})P(C_{ik}=1)}{P(y_i, \mu_k)} \\ \propto P(y_i, mu_k | C_{ik})P(\mu_k)P(C_{ik}=1)\]

due to independence of \(\mu\) and \(C_i\). Then \(P(C_{ik}=1)=\frac{1}{K}\), and using the Gaussian likelihood \(p(y_i|\mu_k, C_{ik}=1)\) and Gaussian prior \(p(\mu_k)\) we obtain that the full conditional for \(C_i\) is proportional to

\[\textrm{exp}(-\frac{1}{2}(y_i-\mu_k)^2 - \frac{1}{2\sigma^2}\mu_k^2)\].

To obtain valid probabilities with which to sample, for every \(y_i\) we compute this expression for each \(\mu_k\) and normalize the resulting \(k\)-length vector by its sum. The sampled label is the update for \(C_ik\) for this iteration.

To obtain the full conditional distribution for \(\mu_k\), follow similar steps as above to obtain

\[p(\mu_k | y_i, C_{ik} = 1) \propto \textrm{exp}(-\frac{1}{2}(y_i-\mu_k)^2 - \frac{1}{2\sigma^2}\mu_k^2)\].

Because the likelihood and the prior of \(\mu_k\) are Gaussian, they yield a Gaussian posterior which we obtain by completing the square:

\[\textrm{exp}(-\frac{1}{2}(y_i-\mu_k)^2 - \frac{1}{2\sigma^2}\mu_k^2) \\ =\textrm{exp}(-\frac{1}{2}((1+\frac{1}{\sigma^2})\mu_k^2-2y_i\mu_k+y_i^2)))\\=\textrm{exp}(-\frac{1}{2}(1+\frac{1}{\sigma^2})(\mu_k^2-\frac{2y_i\mu_k}{1+\frac{1}{\sigma^2}}+(\frac{y_i}{1+\frac{1}{\sigma^2}})^2-(\frac{y_i}{1+\frac{1}{\sigma^2}})^2+y_i^2))) \\ \propto \textrm{exp}(-\frac{1}{2}(1+\frac{1}{\sigma^2})(\mu_k - \frac{y_i}{1+\frac{1}{\sigma^2}})^2)\]

which implies \(\mu_k | y_i, C_{ik} \sim \textrm{N}(\frac{y_i}{1+\frac{1}{\sigma^2}}, \frac{\sigma^2}{\sigma^2+1})\).

The update for \(mu_k\) is then sampled from this distribution.

To fit the Old Faithful waiting time data set as a Gaussian 2-mixture, we use the derived CAVI algorithm and Gibbs Sampler with 3 chains of 10000 samples with 2000 burn-in samples. The estimates for \(\mu_1\), \(\mu_2\), and computation time are given below.
